University of Pittsbugh Medical Center

CMU MHCI Capstone Project

As the UX Design Lead for the Capstone project, I participated in data gathering, data synthesis. I also created the concept and visual identity for the project, and was responsible for the visuals for our deliverables including our research report, presentation slides and weekly communication materials.

Overview

The University of Pittsburgh Medical Center (UPMC) is an integrated global non-profit health enterprise that has more than 62,000 employees, 22 hospitals, and a 2.2 million-member health insurance division. They are also ranked one of top ten hospitals in the United States.

For our CMU MHCI Capstone Project, our team is working with UPMC to design a patient-facing tool with the goals of deeper engagement with the health system and better health outcomes.

During our initial research, we conducted a series of guerrilla interviews, literature review and competitive analysis to gain an overview of the healthcare landscape and to inform more formal studies.

Semi-structured Interviews

After gaining an initial understanding of the healthcare landscape, we conducted a number of more focused interviews with patients and clinicians that are more in line with our objectives. These interviews were more structured and helped us deepen our understanding in the area of interest.

Story Sharing

Using the information gathered in the interviews, we held story sharing sessions to keep the team updated on what each member had found in the field. We then used the insights to build an affinity diagram to see the recurring themes and overarching issues.

Diary Studies And House Visits

In order to validate some of our findings, and to dig deeper into some of the questions we had, we conducted a one week long diary study on some of the participants we interviewed. To explore the surrounding context, we went through the diary studies with participants. For some of these participants, we made on-site visits to see first-hand the environment they lived in.

Data Consolidation

After gathering the data from our background as well as field research, we consolidated and synthesized them to generate higher level insights, created experience maps and cultural models to gain a holistic view of the problem space.

Personas and Visioning

Backed by our background and field research, we developed two personas we felt represented the patients we were trying to help. These personas, along with our key findings, kept us focused as we created more than 50 visions that we believed would address the problem.
